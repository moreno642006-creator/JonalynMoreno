GROWTH AND YIELD PERFORMANCE OF DIFFERENT LOWLAND RICE VARIETIES GROWN UNDER MIGARA, CALBAYOG CONDITION

GROWTH AND YIELD PERFORMANCE OF DIFFERENT LOWLAND RICE VARIETIES GROWN UNDER MIGARA, CALBAYOG CONDITION

CHAPTER 1

INTRODUCTION

Nature and importance of the study

Rice (oryza sativa linn.) has been grown for its varied uses. over 96% of the world rice crop is used for human food (McGraw-Hill, 1968). the rice brand is an exellent source of a major ingredient in manufacturing food products such as puff rice, flakes and native delicacies. Moreover, rice by product are used as ingredient for animals feeds and pet food, edible oil, cooking and other products (Lesam, 1991)

Generally rice is cultivated in the philippines under lowland and upland conditioins. there are 3.5 million hectar of farmland devoted rice production. ofthese total land area, 1.1 million hectares are cultivated to wet season irrigation rice, 0.6 million hectares for rain fed, and 0.4 million hectares for upland rice culture (IRRI, 1992)

Most lowland areas in the country are already under rice cultivation and many researches are concentrated on lowland rice cultivation. Many improvements on cultural management practices have been done on lowland rice to increase production however it was just recently varietal improvement was studied since this play a major role in the total producton of rice(Giason, et, al. 1979)

The tremendous advances in genetics, plan breeding and other closely related fields of discipline have radically changed the concept of cultivaras improvement of the different species of crops, which includes rice. Research studies have been done to find out the high yielding and good quality of rice variety possessing desired agronomic characteristics such as resistace to pests and diseases and adaptable to soil (IRRI, 1992).

Sales (1996) said that productivity has always considered as a key measures of performance in any agricultural system. Since the agricultural system plays a very vital role in the economic must be increased in order to support the level of consumption of the growing population. One of the problem in our country and possess a great challenge involved in research is to generate appropriate technologies all over the country, as well as in extension activities.

It is not enough that the farmers know the art of cultivating the crops. They must also know the proper actual practices to increase rice production. That is why rice production is already given much importance these days, infact, it has received the top support of the government because the supply of rice as a staple food now a days cannot meet the needs of the countries population (IRRI, 1985). Thus, to, meet the demand for increase rice production there is need to boost its production by maintaning the proper management of growing rice.

This study was conducted in order to generate information on the performance oil different lowland rice varieties planted under Migara calbayog conditon.

Objective of the study

1. To evaluate the growth and yield performance of their different lowland rice varieties (PSB Rc18,NSIC Rc112).

2. To compare the cost and return analysis of growing different lowland rice varieties;and

3. To determine what specific variety that would give high yeild.

Scope and limitation

This study focused oil the growth and yeild performance of the these different lowland rice varieties grown under migara, calbayog city conditon. The different varieties that were used where as follows: PSB Rc18, NSIC Rc110, NSIC Rc112.

Time and place of the study

This study was conducted at Brgy. Migara, Calbayog City from September 3, 2005 to January 4,2006.
